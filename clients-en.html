Case Studies

Explore our successful project implementations

Air Quality Assessment for Industrial Complex

Industry: Manufacturing

SECA conducted a comprehensive air quality assessment for a major industrial complex, involving dispersion modeling, monitoring station setup, and regulatory compliance analysis.

The project involved installation of meteorological stations, data analysis spanning multiple years, and sophisticated mathematical modeling using AERMOD, CALPUFF, and proprietary SECA methodologies.

Results: The client successfully demonstrated regulatory compliance, optimized operations, and implemented cost-effective emissions control strategies.

Environmental Impact Assessment for Energy Plant

Industry: Energy

For a new energy generation facility, SECA developed a comprehensive environmental impact assessment with a focus on air quality and meteorological conditions.

Our team conducted baseline measurements, implemented advanced dispersion models, and provided recommendations for emissions control technologies.

Results: The project received environmental licensing approval with minimal modifications, saving the client significant time and resources in the approval process.

Urban Air Quality Monitoring Network

Industry: Public Sector

SECA designed and implemented an integrated air quality monitoring network for a major urban center, combining fixed and mobile monitoring stations.

The project included sensor selection, station placement optimization, data management infrastructure, and public reporting systems.

Results: The system provided real-time air quality data for public health initiatives and enabled targeted traffic management measures that reduced pollution in critical areas.

Emergency Response Planning for Chemical Facility

Industry: Chemical

SECA developed atmospheric dispersion modeling scenarios to support emergency response planning for a chemical manufacturing facility.

The work included identification of potential release scenarios, modeling of dispersion under various meteorological conditions, and development of response protocols.

Results: The facility improved emergency preparedness, developed targeted evacuation plans, and reduced potential response times for hazardous material releases.
