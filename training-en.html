Specialized Environmental Training

SECA offers comprehensive training programs designed for professionals in the environmental sector, regulatory agencies, industries, and academic institutions. Our training courses provide essential knowledge and practical skills needed to understand and manage environmental challenges related to air quality and climate.

Led by experienced professionals with extensive industry knowledge, our programs combine theoretical foundations with hands-on practical sessions, ensuring participants gain applicable skills they can immediately implement in their professional environments.

We provide both standard courses and customized training solutions tailored to the specific needs of your organization. All our training programs can be delivered at your facility, our training center, or virtually through online platforms.

Air Dispersion Modeling Courses

Introduction to Atmospheric Dispersion Modeling

Duration: 3 days

This introductory course provides the fundamental concepts of atmospheric dispersion and how mathematical models are used to predict the transport and dispersion of air pollutants. Participants will learn about the basic principles of meteorology, dispersion mechanisms, and how these are represented in models.

Topics covered:

  • Atmospheric physics basics
  • Dispersion mechanisms
  • Types of dispersion models
  • Input data requirements
  • Model selection criteria
  • Interpretation of results

Audience: Environmental professionals, regulators, consultants

Level: Beginner

Format: Lectures and demonstrations

Advanced AERMOD Modeling Workshop

Duration: 5 days

This intensive workshop provides comprehensive training on the AERMOD modeling system, focusing on advanced techniques and problem-solving. Participants will work through real-world case studies and gain hands-on experience with AERMOD and its preprocessors.

Topics covered:

  • AERMOD model structure and algorithms
  • AERMET meteorological data processing
  • AERMAP terrain processing
  • Building downwash with BPIP-PRIME
  • Advanced source characterization
  • Model evaluation and sensitivity analysis
  • Regulatory applications and reporting

Audience: Air quality modelers, consultants

Level: Intermediate to Advanced

Format: Lectures and hands-on exercises

Register Interest

CALPUFF Modeling System Training

Duration: 4 days

This course provides comprehensive training on the CALPUFF modeling system, a non-steady-state puff dispersion model that simulates the effects of time- and space-varying meteorological conditions on pollution transport, transformation, and removal.

Topics covered:

  • CALPUFF model structure and capabilities
  • CALMET meteorological model
  • Geophysical data preprocessing
  • Long-range transport modeling
  • Complex terrain applications
  • Chemical transformation mechanisms
  • Postprocessing with CALPOST

Audience: Experienced modelers

Level: Advanced

Format: Lectures and hands-on exercises

Environmental Monitoring Courses

Air Quality Monitoring Systems

Duration: 3 days

This course provides a comprehensive overview of air quality monitoring systems, including equipment selection, station design, data collection, and quality assurance. Participants will learn both theoretical principles and practical aspects of maintaining effective monitoring programs.

Topics covered:

  • Monitoring objectives and network design
  • Equipment selection and specifications
  • Installation and calibration procedures
  • Data acquisition and management systems
  • Quality assurance/quality control protocols
  • Data analysis and reporting

Audience: Environmental technicians, engineers

Level: Intermediate

Format: Lectures and equipment demonstrations

Meteorological Monitoring for Environmental Applications

Duration: 2 days

This course focuses on meteorological monitoring systems used for environmental applications, particularly those supporting air quality studies and dispersion modeling. Participants will learn about instrumentation, data collection, and best practices for generating high-quality meteorological data.

Topics covered:

  • Meteorological parameters and their significance
  • Instruments and sensor technologies
  • Tower design and installation
  • Data logger programming and configuration
  • Data validation techniques
  • Preparing meteorological data for dispersion models

Audience: Environmental professionals, technicians

Level: Intermediate

Format: Lectures and field exercises

Regulatory Compliance Courses

Environmental Impact Assessment for Air Quality

Duration: 3 days

This course provides guidance on preparing air quality components of environmental impact assessments (EIAs). Participants will learn methodologies for characterizing baseline conditions, evaluating impacts, and developing mitigation measures in accordance with regulatory requirements.

Topics covered:

  • Regulatory framework and requirements
  • Baseline characterization methodologies
  • Impact prediction techniques
  • Significance criteria and evaluation
  • Mitigation measure development
  • Monitoring program design
  • Report preparation and submission

Audience: Environmental consultants, industry professionals

Level: Intermediate to Advanced

Format: Lectures and case studies

Air Quality Permitting Workshop

Duration: 2 days

This workshop guides participants through the air quality permitting process, focusing on the technical requirements and documentation needed for successful permit applications. Regulatory requirements, emission calculations, control technology assessments, and demonstration of compliance will be covered.

Topics covered:

  • Emission inventory development
  • Best Available Control Technology (BACT) analysis
  • Air quality impact analysis
  • Compliance demonstration techniques
  • Permit application preparation
  • Permit conditions and compliance monitoring
